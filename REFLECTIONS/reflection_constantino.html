Reflection Paper

Janna M. Constantino

It was difficult but enjoyable to create our website, PooCafe, especially since I was the primary writer. I first underestimated the challenge of creating a consistent and appealing brand voice. In order to be brief and clear, I had to work on honing my voice, which required practice and feedback.

Writing thorough product descriptions made me reflect carefully on what we have to offer and how we may engage clients. One important thing I discovered was how much readability is impacted by design decisions. I learned a lot about visual communication by experimenting with text sizes, selecting appropriate fonts, and using headers and subheadings to arrange content.

The construction of PooCafe has brought to light how all facets of web design are interrelated. Writing engaging content and producing eye-catching images are only two aspects of it, the key is how these components combine to form a brand identity that appeals to our target market.

I learned the hard way that designs that seem fantastic on a laptop might not work on a phone, which made me remember to test them on a variety of devices.

All in all, this experience taught me the value of precision, flexibility, and clarity in all facets of creating a digital experience, not only in writing.
